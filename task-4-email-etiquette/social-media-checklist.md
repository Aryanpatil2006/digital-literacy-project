# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.
2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" before publishing any content.
3. **Share your achievements** – Post about certifications, projects, internships, and academic milestones to build a positive digital footprint.
4. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review and update privacy settings on personal accounts to control who sees your content.
5. **Engage respectfully** – Comment thoughtfully, give credit when sharing others' work, and participate in constructive academic or professional discussions.
6. **Follow thought leaders and industry pages** – Use social media as a learning tool by following educators, researchers, and companies in your field.
7. **Report cyberbullying or harassment** – Use the platform's reporting tools and seek support if you or someone you know is being targeted.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't post offensive, hateful, or discriminatory content** – This includes jokes targeting religion, caste, gender, or ethnicity. Such posts can have serious academic and legal consequences.
2. **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or financial details on any public platform.
3. **Don't spread misinformation** – Always verify a news item or statistic before sharing it. Forwarding fake news, even unintentionally, harms your credibility.
4. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** – Neither participate in trolling others nor stay silent when you witness harassment online.
5. **Don't use social media during exams or important academic tasks** – Excessive scrolling affects focus, productivity, and academic performance.
6. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from unknown individuals** – Especially on personal accounts; this is a common first step in social engineering attacks.
7. **Don't post about others without their consent** – Sharing someone's photo, location, or personal situation without permission is a violation of their privacy and digital rights.
