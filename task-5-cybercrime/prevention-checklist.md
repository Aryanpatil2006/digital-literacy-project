# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** – Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms. Use a password manager if needed.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** – Turn on 2FA for all important accounts (email, social media, banking apps). This adds an extra layer of security beyond just your password.

3. **Verify before you click** – Never click on links in suspicious emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages. Check the sender's full email address and look for spelling mistakes or unofficial domains.

4. **Keep your apps and devices updated** – Software updates often contain critical security patches. Enable automatic updates for your phone's operating system and apps.

5. **Use secure Wi-Fi connections** – Avoid using public/unsecured Wi-Fi for banking, logging into accounts, or sharing personal data. Use mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

6. **Be cautious with app permissions** – Review what permissions (camera, contacts, location) an app requests before installing. Deny permissions that seem unnecessary.

---

## Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN with anyone** – No bank, UPI app, or government agency will ever ask for your PIN, OTP, or password. Anyone who does is a fraudster.

8. **Understand Collect Requests – NEVER approve them from strangers** – A collect request takes money FROM you, not sends it TO you. If someone says "I am sending you money, just approve this request," it is a scam. Entering your PIN on a collect request means you are paying them.

9. **Verify the receiver's UPI ID before transferring funds** – Always double-check the name that appears after entering a UPI ID before confirming any transaction, even to people you know.

10. **Set transaction limits on your UPI app** – Most banking apps allow you to set a daily UPI transaction limit. Keep this limit low unless you need a larger transaction temporarily.

---

## Identity & Privacy Protection

11. **Do not post personal details publicly** – Avoid sharing your Aadhaar number, PAN card, bank account details, college ID, or phone number on social media or public platforms.

12. **Report cybercrime immediately** – If you are a victim of any online fraud, cyberbullying, or harassment, report it at:
    - 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal**: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
    - 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline**: **1930** (available 24x7 across India)
    - Report as quickly as possible – for financial fraud, the sooner you report, the higher the chance of transaction reversal.
